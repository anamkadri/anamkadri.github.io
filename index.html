About Me

My aim as a writer is to inspire positive change, spark critical thinking, and engage in meaningful dialogue over the topics I write about.

As someone who has a keen interest in reading critical analyses on media, literature, and theory, I too like to express my opinions and observations through words on paper. I am moved by the unique characteristics of human beings and their interactions with one another and with the world around them. I like to observe human habits through analyzing media texts, be it the mundanity of everyday life or the complicated social dynamics of the world. For this reason, I mainly gravitate towards non-fiction writing, composing pieces of analyses that reflect my observations, or creative non-fiction that appeals to my emotions. I have a strong sense of social justice and much of my writing calls out systemic structures and ideologies of oppression that I observe in day-to-day life or in the media I consume, such as my piece Disrupting Societal Hegemonies in Wadjda (2012) by Haifaa al-Mansour.

As a writer in the digital sphere who is artistic and who values authenticity, I have a knack for design to give my work character that is reflective of me. I channel my creativity by organizing multimodal texts on the appropriate channels in a captivating way, such as designing presentations, documents, posters, websites, and more that reflect my personality and values for communication in a neat and engaging manner.

My Work Samples

Media Analyses on Films

  • Disrupting Societal Hegemonies in Wadjda (2012) by Haifaa al-Mansour

    I analyze the filmic techniques al-Mansour deploys in her film to disrupt the societal hegemonies of a patriarchal society.

  • The Male Gaze and the Portrayal of Natasha Romanoff in the Marvel Cinematic Universe

    I explore the use of the male gaze in the MCU when portraying Natasha Romanoff and the changes Director Cate Shortland brings to the character after removing the convention.

  • Historical Factors Show How Themes of Career and Romance Evolve from George Cukor’s to Greta Gerwig’s Adaptation of Little Women (1868) by Louisa May Alcott

    I explore how women's career and romance have evolved from the 1930s to the 2010s in filmic adaptations of Alcott's famous novel.

Website Development and Research

  • Marketing Horror Films: A Film Posters and Trailers Genre Analysis

    I conducted a group analysis on the marketing capabilities of the film posters and trailers genres, and designed the website using WordPress to showcase our research.

  • Problems within Stan Culture

    I conducted an analysis on the harmful aspects of Stan Culture and possible solutions, and designed a website using WordPress to showcase my research.
